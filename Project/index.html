Welcome to the amazing world of cinema!

The Big Cinema is a website dedicated to the world of cinema, exploring its history, evolution and cultural impact. It presents the main professions of the seventh art and highlights the great names that have marked world cinema.

Check out our content!

Writers

Writer

Screenwriters are the lifeblood of cinema - they turn ideas into stories, creating dialog, scenes and structures that bring films to life. Without them, there would be no narrative.

Directors

Director

Directors are the visionaries of cinema – they shape the tone, rhythm, and emotional core of a film, transforming scripts into immersive visual experiences.

Actors

Actor

Composers

Composer

Editors

Editor

Editors are the architects of rhythm in cinema – they shape the narrative by weaving together images, sounds, and timing to create flow and impact.

Cinema History

Cinema was born at the end of the 19th century with the Lumière brothers evolved from silent shorts to large sound productions. Throughout the 20th century, it became a powerful form of art and cultural expression. Today, it continues to enchant generations with technology, creativity and emotion.

The Cinématographe, created by the Lumière brothers in 1895, was a revolutionary camera that also functioned as a projector and film copier.

The first film by the Lumière brothers, La Sortie de l'Usine Lumière à Lyon (1895), shows workers leaving a factory and is a cinema milestone.

The first Oscar ceremony took place on May 16, 1929, in Hollywood, honoring films from 1927 to 1928.
