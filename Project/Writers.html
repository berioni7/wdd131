Writers

Screenwriter is the professional responsible for creating the script for films, series, plays or other audiovisual productions. They develop dialogues, scenes and narrative structures that guide the entire production. Their importance lies in transforming ideas into captivating stories that move and connect the audience. Without the scriptwriter, there is no story to be told. It is the creative starting point of every work.

Main responsibilities of a writer:

  • Create and develop the story – Develop engaging plots with coherent characters, conflicts and outcomes.
  • Writing authentic dialogue – Producing lines that sound natural and reveal the characters' personalities.
  • Structuring the script – Organizing the narrative into acts, scenes and sequences, maintaining rhythm and dramatic progression.
  • Adapting ideas or existing works – Transforming books, real events or ideas into film or television scripts.
  • Collaborating with the production team – Working with directors, producers and editors to adjust the script to the needs of the production.

Top Nine Screenwriters in Cinema:

1.

Robert Towne

Robert Towne is a renowned American screenwriter, famous for his work on *Chinatown* (1974), which won him the Oscar for Best Original Screenplay. He also wrote The Last Detail (1973) and Shampoo (1975). His writing is sophisticated, with precise dialog and a noir atmosphere.

2.

Aaron Sorkin

Aaron Sorkin is known for his dense scripts full of quick, intelligent dialog. He wrote *The Social Network* (2010), which won him an Oscar, as well as *A Few Good Men* (1992) and *Moneyball* (2011). His style emphasizes verbal rhythm and intense moral debates.

3.

Eric Roth

Eric Roth is a master of emotionally charged scripts on an epic scale. He won an Oscar for Forrest Gump (1994), and also wrote The Curious Case of Benjamin Button (2008) and Dune (2021). His writing balances sentiment and grandeur.

4.

Melissa Mathison

Melissa Mathison was famous for writing E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982), one of the most exciting screenplays in the history of cinema. She also wrote The Black Stallion (1979) and Kundun (1997). Her sensitivity gave soul to magical stories.

5.

Charlie Kaufman

Charlie Kaufman, at the beginning of his career, was only a screenwriter, creating works such as Being John Malkovich (1999), Adaptation (2002) and Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind (2004). Known for his inventive and psychological style.

6.

Alvin Sargent

Alvin Sargent has written award-winning and popular scripts such as Ordinary People (1980), Spider-Man 2 (2004) and Julia (1977). He stands out for his solid structure and emotional sensitivity. He has never ventured into directing.

7.

Ruth Prawer Jhabvala

Ruth Prawer Jhabvala collaborated with the famous Merchant-Ivory duo on films such as A Room with a View (1985), Howards End (1992) and The Remains of the Day (1993). His scripts are refined, sensitive and always literary.

8.

David Webb Peoples

David Webb Peoples has written outstanding works such as Blade Runner (1982), Unforgiven (1992) and 12 Monkeys (1995). His scripts are philosophical and dense, with complex worlds. He has never directed his stories.

9.

Jean-Claude Carrière

Jean-Claude Carrière was Buñuel's faithful partner, scripting films such as Belle de Jour (1967) and The Discreet Charm of the Bourgeoisie (1972). He also wrote The Unbearable Lightness of Being (1988). Elegant, surreal and profound style.
